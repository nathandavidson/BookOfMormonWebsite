How Beautiful Are They

Mosiah 18:30

Posted by Nathan Davidson on April 14, 2023

I have a good friend I taught on my mission who is seriously considering baptism. She has had a lot of struggles in her life, more than most to be honest. However, through learning the gospel and coming to love Jesus Christ, she has come to see these traumatic past experiences and every other circumstance of her life through the lense of the gospel. With that new perspective, tinted a brighter shade by the pure love of our savior, she thinks many things are beautiful now that weren’t before.

I am reminded of the baptism account given in Mosiah 18. Though it is likely that the waters of mormon were not much more than a seasonal watering hole for wild animals -

"And it came to pass that as many as did believe him did go forth to a place which was called Mormon, having received its name from the king, being in the borders of the land having been infested, by times or at seasons, by wild beasts."

The light of the gospel tinted those baptized saints perspective and they saw the place as beautiful.

"And now it came to pass that all this was done in Mormon, yea, by the waters of Mormon, in the forest that was near the waters of Mormon; yea, the place of Mormon, the waters of Mormon, the forest of Mormon, how beautiful are they to the eyes of them who there came to the knowledge of their Redeemer; yea, and how blessed are they, for they shall sing to his praise forever."

I wrote this short melody and it strikes me as fitting the mood and perspective of those saints who, at the waters of mormon, “came to the knowledge of their redeemer.”
